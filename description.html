~ How the scavenger hunt works ~

The scavenger hunt is an outdoor experience designed to let you become a detective for a day. It is great for friends, family, or team-building.

First, gather together your best detectives. The course works best with a team of 3-6 people.

Next, reserve a spot and meet at Town Common Park. Your team will receive a binder filled with a map and clues. Visit the designated areas on the map and figure out how to use the clues to solve the puzzle associated with that location.

Your team will race against 2-8 other teams. The puzzles may be solved in any order, but in an effort to spread out the teams and avoid interference, your team will be assigned a starting location. Visit the remaining locations in any order you wish.

The difficulty of puzzles is challenging but rewarding. While children will be able to understand some clues, the majority of the puzzles are suited for adults. Once you've solved each puzzle, you will be able to decipher the final solution and solve the crime.

The course is located in downtown Greenville, including the campus of East Carolina University (ECU). Visiting each location will require you to walk 3-4 miles. Visit each location if you're spry, or divy up the puzzles amongst your team if you must. It takes 2-4 hours on average to solve all of the clues.

For those with time constraints, a shorter version of the scavenger hunt is available. You will still be able to solve the mystery and discover the missing child's whereabouts in the shortened version, but you will be ineligible to win first place in the team competition.
