~ How the scavenger hunt works ~

A child is missing! Become a detective, investigate the lovely Greenville area, get some exercise, and solve a mystery.

The scavenger hunt is an outdoor experience designed to highlight Greenville's landmarks. It is great for friends, family, or team-building.

First, gather together your best detectives. The course works best with a team of 3-6 people.

Next, reserve a spot and meet at Town Common Park. Your team will receive a binder with a map and clues. Visit the designated spots on the map and use the clues to solve the puzzle associated with that location. Once you've solved each puzzle, you will be able to decipher the final solution and solve the crime.

Your team will race against 2-8 other teams. Your team will be assigned a starting location to spread out competing teams, but after that you may visit the locations in any order you choose.

The difficulty of puzzles is challenging but rewarding. While children will be able to understand some clues, the majority of the puzzles are suited for adults.

The course is located in downtown Greenville, including the campus of East Carolina University (ECU). Visiting each location will require you to walk 3-4 miles. Visit each location if you're spry, or divy up the puzzles amongst your team if you must. It takes 2-4 hours on average to solve all of the clues.

For those with time constraints, a shorter version of the scavenger hunt is available. You will still be able to discover the missing child's whereabouts in the shortened version, but you will be ineligible to win first place in the team competition.

INVESTIGATE. EXERCISE. SOLVE.
